ABOUT CATS

The cat is a domesticated species of small carnivorous mammal. In order to distinguish it from the family's wild relatives, it is the only domesticated species in the Felidae genus and is frequently referred to as the domestic cat or house cat. Cats are frequently kept as home pets, but they can also be farm cats or feral cats; the latter roams freely and stays away from people. The smallest member of the Felidae family and domesticated member of the order Carnivora, they are also known as house cats or domestic cats. Domestic cats, like all felids, have supple, low-slung bodies, delicately shaped heads, long tails that help with balance, and specialized fangs and claws that make them ideally suited to an active hunting lifestyle. In addition to being primarily carnivorous, highly agile and powerful, and well-coordinated in their movements, cats share other characteristics with their wild predecessors.
